Product definition

The Ghostwriter experience

A live creative studio for full novels and multi-book story worlds where authors, editors, rich visual planning, version history, and accountable assistants work together.

Product thesis

Ghostwriter is the trusted operating system for a story—not an AI book generator and not a chat window beside a document.

The primary product connects the work a novelist otherwise spreads across word processors, notes, wikis, index cards, whiteboards, versioned files, editorial email, and generic AI chats. The manuscript and spatial Story Canvas are linked views of the same books, scenes, characters, world, and arcs. Git-inspired history makes exploration safe without asking a writer to understand Git.

Accepted first promise: “Bring Ghostwriter an idea, a draft, or a whole story world. Develop it visually, write the book, collaborate with editors and assistants, explore alternate versions, and keep every decision understandable across the full series.”

The “Spotify for creative stories” analogy is useful for eventual reach and discovery, but not as an early product or ownership model. Authoring, retention, export, rights, moderation, licensing, and audience demand must be proven before a centralized marketplace deserves investment.

Who it serves

Initial beachhead

The novelist

Is creating a full book with many scenes, threads, revisions, and editorial decisions. Needs deep project memory, visual and prose workflows, safe experimentation, and a dependable path to a finished edition.

Hire Ghostwriter to: hold the whole novel together while preserving direct creative control.

Initial beachhead

The aspiring novelist

Has scenes, characters, worlds, or a premise but not yet a repeatable book-writing process. Needs a useful next step, editable scaffolds, and craft guidance without prompt expertise or creative takeover.

Hire Ghostwriter to: turn a bold idea into a complete novel while learning a process that still feels like theirs.

Designed in from start

The series storyteller

Maintains multiple books, lore, timelines, characters, relationships, rules, and arcs across a growing canon. Needs book-level boundaries and project-wide story truth.

Hire Ghostwriter to: make a bold multi-book world navigable, coherent, and reusable.

Core collaborator

The editor

Needs an invited, attributable way to comment, suggest tracked changes, compare alternatives, request revision, and review a named version without taking over the author’s workspace.

Hire Ghostwriter to: keep editorial collaboration connected to the exact prose, context, and decision history.

Industry horizon

The publisher, room, or studio

Needs revision-pinned submissions, organizational roles, rights and provenance, interchange, adaptation workflows, and operational controls beyond author/editor collaboration.

Entry condition: creators already finish and voluntarily share meaningful work through Ghostwriter.

Cross-cutting power user: an agent-enabled creator uses Cursor, Claude, or another MCP client against a deliberately scoped project. MCP is strategic infrastructure, not the first acquisition message.

Project scale and creative hierarchy

Story project / universe→ One or more books→ Parts and chapters→ Scenes→ Passages and blocks

Characters, places, world rules, series timeline, research, and cross-book arcs may live at project scope. A book owns its manuscript order, book-specific outline, edition history, and release state. Scenes remain the primary writing and review unit while stable block IDs anchor comments, links, evidence, and tracked suggestions.

Git-inspired, writer-facing history

  • Working draft: live autosaved state shared with authorized collaborators.
  • Checkpoint: immutable recovery point created automatically or by the writer.
  • Variant: an alternate scene, passage, chapter, or book direction created without disturbing the current draft.
  • Compare and combine: inspect differences, accept selected changes, and resolve conflicts through prose-aware review.
  • Review request: invite an editor to a named scope and revision with comments and tracked suggestions.
  • Edition: a named, immutable book or series snapshot suitable for export, submission, or publication.

These concepts borrow Git’s safety and traceability, not its interface. Writers should never need to stage files, understand commit hashes, or resolve raw textual merge markers.

Jobs to be done

Capture without losing the spark

When an idea arrives in fragments, help me preserve it immediately and decide what to do with it later.

Find the story inside the idea

When I know what feels exciting but not what holds it together, ask useful questions and show editable possibilities.

Know what happens next

When I am stuck, reconnect me to character intent, scene purpose, setup, and consequences instead of producing generic filler.

Hold the whole project

When the manuscript becomes too large for working memory, surface relevant facts, promises, arcs, and prior events with evidence.

Revise without losing myself

When I need another perspective, offer bounded alternatives that preserve voice, explain tradeoffs, and never overwrite silently.

Finish and leave freely

When the project is ready, help me make deliberate passes, create a stable snapshot, and export an editable work I still own.

Experience principles

  1. Artifact before assistant. A scene, character, timeline event, finding, or proposal is the durable result. Conversation is attached context, not the container for the work.
  2. One story model. Draft, Story Canvas, outline, timeline, story bible, and review use the same books, scenes, and entities; the product never maintains drifting visual, prose, or AI copies.
  3. Guidance is a dial. Guided, Balanced, and Minimal modes change pacing, explanation, and defaults—not the underlying project or feature access.
  4. Craft is a lens, not a law. Explain why a question may help, allow “not relevant,” support custom structures, and never grade creativity against one formula.
  5. Evidence earns trust. Continuity and craft findings cite the passages and project facts that produced them, state uncertainty, and can be marked intentional.
  6. The draft stays quiet. Writing is the primary action. Agent tools appear at selection, scene, review, and assignment boundaries rather than interrupting every paragraph.
  7. Every generated change is provisional. The writer can compare, edit, accept selectively, reject, undo, and inspect who or what produced it.
  8. Collaboration is attributable. Every comment, suggestion, accepted change, variant, and edition identifies its human or agent origin and the author/editor who approved it.
  9. History supports courage. Checkpoints and variants make bold experimentation cheap while merges remain prose-aware and reviewable.
  10. Ownership is observable. Server acknowledgement, connection/recovery state, network egress, export, provider, and collaborator/agent access are visible in plain language.
  11. Resume beats gamification. Restore context and suggest a useful next move; do not use streaks, guilt, or words-generated metrics as the core reward.

The project truth model

Writers need to know not only what Ghostwriter knows, but how that knowledge became authoritative. These states must be visually and technically distinct.

Authored

Accepted manuscript text and writer-authored notes. Direct human edits autosave as working state and become restorable revisions.

Planned

Intent that may happen: outline beats, future facts, scene goals, arc hypotheses, and creative constraints.

Confirmed

Writer-approved canon: entities, facts, rules, relationships, and timeline events, preferably linked to evidence.

Inferred

Evidence-backed observations from text. Useful for review, never promoted to canon just because a model is confident.

Proposed

Agent drafts, edits, findings, new facts, and structural changes waiting in the Inbox. They cannot affect canonical work until applied.

Permanent rule: automatic synchronization means “detect and prepare a reviewable update,” not “silently rewrite the story bible or manuscript.”

Assistance modes

Guided

One useful decision at a time, examples in the writer’s genre, plain-language craft explanations, recommended next steps, and small proposal batches.

Balanced

Contextual prompts and optional structure with direct access to every artifact. The default for creators who want a collaborator, not a course.

Minimal

Blank-project entry, compact chrome, keyboard-first navigation, direct metadata editing, custom templates, and agent actions only on request.

Mode is set per project and can change at any time. It must never affect export, ownership, model quality, or access to advanced tools.

From spark to finished work

  1. Enter from anywhere

    Choose Start with a spark, Import pages, or Start blank. Preserve the original input unchanged and create a project immediately; no mandatory setup gauntlet.

  2. Set a creative compass

    Capture the format, audience, genre promise, emotional intent, creative boundaries, and desired assistance level. Every field is editable and skippable.

  3. Shape the story engine

    Explore protagonist desire, opposition, stakes, change, dramatic question, and world pressure through one question at a time. Agent outputs are editable seed cards.

  4. Build only the structure that helps

    Create characters, places, rules, relationships, a timeline, beats, or scene cards. Choose a familiar structural lens, a custom structure, or discovery writing.

  5. Draft in scenes

    Write in a focused editor, storyboard on the Canvas, or use both in split view. Scene intent and linked project context stay nearby; agent actions are explicit and scoped to a selection, scene, region, or assignment.

  6. Curate the living story

    Ghostwriter detects candidate entities, facts, timeline changes, promises, and contradictions. The writer confirms, edits, rejects, or leaves them inferred.

  7. Review in deliberate passes

    Invite editors into a named scope, compare variants, and run continuity, character, structure, pacing, voice, and line-level passes separately. Human and agent suggestions share a transparent review surface.

  8. Finish a stable edition

    Resolve or intentionally defer findings, merge the chosen variants, create a named edition, inspect readiness, and preserve the exact book and project revisions used.

  9. Take it anywhere

    Export readable Markdown and publishable formats plus a lossless Ghostwriter archive. AI availability or subscription status must never block access to authored work.

The embedded craft system

“Best practices” should become optional, contextual playbooks rather than a giant questionnaire. Each playbook teaches through a creative decision, writes to shared project artifacts, and produces something the writer can keep.

Frameworks are selectable lenses, not hidden scoring rubrics. The system must support nonlinear narratives, unreliable narrators, intentional contradictions, experimental forms, and “none of these” as first-class choices.

Core product loops

Draft loop

Orient → write → autosave locally → checkpoint → resume with the relevant scene intent and context.

Canon loop

Detect candidate fact → show source → confirm, revise, reject, or dispute → update the story model.

Craft loop

Declare intent → create → inspect against that intent → choose a targeted suggestion → keep the writer’s decision.

Agent loop

Assign goal and scope → inspect context receipt → generate one or more variants → compare evidence and prose → combine, accept selectively, or send back.

Version loop

Checkpoint → branch a variant → explore freely → compare → combine or restore → preserve the decision in history.

Editorial loop

Request review at a named revision → comment and suggest → discuss in context → author resolves → edition records attribution.

Completion loop

Choose revision pass → resolve or defer findings → snapshot → export → continue into another edition if desired.

North-star behavior: weekly advancing projects—projects that add meaningful authored text or complete an intentional planning, review, or export milestone. Words generated is not a success metric.

Representative storyboards

1. Spark to first scene

An emerging creator writes one sentence. Ghostwriter preserves it, asks one high-leverage question, prepares editable premise and character seeds, offers an optional three-scene path, and opens Scene 1 with a plain-language intention. The writer can start typing at every step.

2. Import and understand

An experienced novelist imports a draft. Ghostwriter previews chapter and scene boundaries, then proposes characters and inferred facts with passages attached. The writer fixes structure, confirms only useful facts, switches to Minimal mode, and continues writing.

3. Revise a passage

The writer selects a paragraph and requests “tighten without changing the weary first-person voice.” A context receipt names the selection and voice references. The Inbox shows four hunks; two are accepted, one is edited, and one is rejected before an atomic apply.

4. Resolve continuity

A bounded review finds two conflicting travel times. Both passages and the planned timeline appear side by side. The writer marks one statement as a character’s mistaken belief and applies a proposal to clarify the other. That decision remains in history.

5. Capture then delegate

The writer records an idea in the narrow web view during a brief connection drop. Minimal browser recovery preserves the unacknowledged note, sync confirms it after reconnect, and an external MCP assistant later proposes two linked scene cards with full provenance.

6. Explore three prose versions with an editor

An assistant proposes three revision variants for a difficult scene. The author keeps the current draft untouched, invites an editor to compare the variants at the same base revision, combines one opening with another ending, and records the accepted result with both human decisions and model provenance.

7. Carry an arc across books

A series author opens Book Three and inspects a character’s project-wide arc, confirmed facts, and unresolved promises from Books One and Two. A continuity review can cite earlier books while book-specific planned details remain separate from established series canon.

Discovery before implementation confidence

  • Interview and prototype-test with 12–15 novelists, including at least four aspiring creators who have not finished a book, three series authors, and three editors.
  • Test all three entries—spark, import, and blank—and all three assistance modes against the same artifacts.
  • Measure whether story-knowledge upkeep stays under roughly two minutes per writing session when candidate extraction is available.
  • Validate that writers understand exactly what an agent read and what accepting a proposal will change.
  • Run both a 120,000-word single-book project and a three-book shared-world project through usability, revision, Canvas, and performance spikes.

Founder decisions

  1. Accepted — beachhead: novelists and aspiring novelists creating full books and potentially multi-book arcs.
  2. Accepted — primary product: responsive real-time web with a rich manuscript editor, Story Canvas, notes, version history, and editorial collaboration.
  3. Accepted — content workflow: Git-inspired checkpoints, variants, compare/merge, attribution, review, and editions expressed through writer-facing UX.
  4. Accepted — agent role: assistants may brainstorm, draft, edit, and offer multiple versions, always in tandem with authors and authorized editors.
  5. Accepted — data authority: ADR 0002 sets server-authoritative, online-only v1 projects with minimal browser recovery for unacknowledged work and no complete offline replica.
  6. Accepted — collaboration depth: live project updates, presence, comments, tracked suggestions, and version review ship before simultaneous same-scene editing; one collaborator directly edits a scene body at a time.
  7. Open — import/export: web access is the primary portability experience; choose which external formats are required at launch versus later.
  8. AI posture: choose hosted provider, bring-your-own-key, local model, or a deliberate combination per surface.
  9. Marketplace boundary: affirm that public profiles, discovery, licensing, payments, social mechanics, and rights workflows stay outside the creation v1.
